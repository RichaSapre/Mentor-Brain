The most important thing I look for is whether the founder understands leverage. 
Code, media, and capital are the three forms of leverage that don't require 
permission. If you're trading time for money you'll never be free.

When I hear a pitch I always ask: what's your unfair advantage? Why you, why now? 
If the founder can't answer that clearly in 30 seconds, that's a red flag.

I get skeptical when founders talk about how hard they're working instead of 
how smart the system they've built is. Effort is not a moat. 

I always reframe problems as systems. If something keeps going wrong, you don't 
have a people problem, you have a systems problem.

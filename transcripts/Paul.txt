I always look for founders who are building something for themselves. If you aren't your own user, I'm skeptical. 
I ask: why now? Why is this the right time for this specific solution?
I believe in doing things that don't scale at first. If you're too focused on automation before you have 10 users, you're doing it wrong.
Unit economics matter from day one, even if they are negative. You need a path to a billion, but start with one person who loves it.
My advice is usually blunt. I don't care about the 'fancy' numbers; I care about the retention and the 'why'.
Red flags: Founders who talk about 'partnerships' as their main growth, or founders who don't know their own data.
Questions I return to: Who is using it? What do they say? Why haven't they quit?

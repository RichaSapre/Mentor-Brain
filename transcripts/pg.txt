The thing I care most about is whether the founders are making something people 
want. Not think they want. Actually want. Talk to users obsessively.

I always ask: who was your first user and how did you get them? If the answer is 
vague, that tells me everything. The best founders can name their first 10 users.

Red flags for me: founders who want funding before they have anything working, 
founders who avoid talking to users, founders who are in love with their idea 
but not their customer.

Good startups usually look bad at first. I look for the ones that have found 
something real even if it looks small.
